## Design Context

### Users
Developers who want a quick, private way to understand what they shipped from GitHub history. They may check the app at the end of a day, during a weekly review, or while reconstructing work across repos.

### Brand Personality
Quiet, precise, and journal-like. The app should feel like a trustworthy developer notebook, not an analytics dashboard or a gamified productivity tracker.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use a refined Apple-native SwiftUI surface through Kit941. The histogram and calendar can carry the product identity, with restrained color density and clear day-by-day affordances. Avoid loud SaaS dashboards, generic gradient hero sections, and busy card stacks.

### Design Principles
- Put the calendar and histogram first; they are the product's memory surface.
- Make every generated journal entry traceable to concrete commits.
- Keep GitHub permissions and token state visible and understandable.
- Prefer calm density over decorative chrome.
- Treat Foundation Models as a core eligibility gate, not a cloud fallback.
